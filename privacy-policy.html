Privacy Policy

Your privacy is important to us

Last Updated: January 30, 2026

Sasi Institute of Technology & Engineering ("we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting the privacy and security of your personal information. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, and protect information when you visit our SITE-EMAR 2026 conference website and register for the event.

1. Information We Collect

Personal Information

When you register for SITE-EMAR 2026, we may collect the following personal information:

  • Full name and title
  • Email address and phone number
  • Institutional affiliation and designation
  • Professional credentials and qualifications
  • Paper submission details (if applicable)
  • Dietary preferences and accessibility requirements
  • Payment information for registration fees

Automatically Collected Information

We may automatically collect certain technical information when you visit our website:

  • IP address and browser type
  • Device information and operating system
  • Pages visited and time spent on our website
  • Referring website addresses
  • Cookies and similar tracking technologies

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the collected information for the following purposes:

  • Processing conference registrations and managing attendee records
  • Communicating important conference updates, schedule changes, and announcements
  • Facilitating paper submissions, reviews, and publication processes
  • Arranging accommodation, catering, and special accessibility requirements
  • Issuing certificates of participation and attendance
  • Conducting surveys and gathering feedback to improve future events
  • Complying with legal and regulatory obligations
  • Preventing fraud and ensuring website security

3. Information Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal information to third parties. We may share your information in the following limited circumstances:

  • Conference Partners: With keynote speakers, session chairs, and organizing committee members for conference coordination
  • Service Providers: With trusted third-party vendors who assist with payment processing, email communications, and event management
  • Publication Partners: With journal publishers and indexing services for accepted papers (with author consent)
  • Legal Requirements: When required by law, court order, or government regulations
  • Institutional Requirements: With your affiliated institution for verification purposes (with consent)

4. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures to protect your personal information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures include:

  • Secure Socket Layer (SSL) encryption for data transmission
  • Regular security assessments and vulnerability testing
  • Restricted access to personal information on a need-to-know basis
  • Employee training on data protection and privacy practices
  • Secure backup systems and disaster recovery procedures

5. Your Rights and Choices

You have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Access: Request a copy of the personal information we hold about you
  • Correction: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your personal data (subject to legal requirements)
  • Opt-Out: Unsubscribe from promotional emails at any time
  • Data Portability: Request your data in a structured, commonly used format
  • Withdraw Consent: Withdraw previously given consent for data processing

6. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies to enhance your browsing experience. You can control cookie settings through your browser preferences. Disabling cookies may affect website functionality.

7. Third-Party Links

Our website may contain links to third-party websites. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of these external sites. We encourage you to review their privacy policies before providing any personal information.

8. Children's Privacy

Our conference is intended for professionals and researchers. We do not knowingly collect personal information from individuals under 18 years of age without parental consent.

9. Data Retention

We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this policy, comply with legal obligations, and maintain records for archival purposes. Conference registration data is typically retained for 5 years after the event.

10. International Data Transfers

Your information may be transferred to and processed in countries other than your country of residence. We ensure appropriate safeguards are in place to protect your information in accordance with applicable data protection laws.

11. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. We will notify you of significant changes by posting the updated policy on our website with a revised "Last Updated" date.

12. Contact Us
